## Overview

Patreon's discovery experience relies primarily on a basic search input field — useful if you already know who you're looking for, but limiting for new and existing patrons who want to explore creators across genres. The current system assumes intent. But discovery is rarely intentional; it happens while browsing, through recommendations, through the platforms you already use. We redesigned Patreon's discovery experience to surface relevant creators through personalized, cross-platform recommendations — and introduced Sage, an AI-powered guide that helps users find creators they didn't know they were looking for.

---

## Design Goals

1. **Surface relevant creators through personalized recommendations** that feel organic, not overwhelming
2. **Encourage exploration** by creating a low-friction, curiosity-driven browsing experience
3. **Build trust through transparent suggestions** and meaningful context behind each recommendation
4. **Support creator growth** by helping the right audiences find and connect with them faster

---

## Research

Three insights shaped the design direction:

**Discovery is Often Algorithm-Led.** Discovery typically happens passively — while browsing — not through intentional searching. Users don't go to Patreon to "find someone new." They stumble across creators through other platforms and arrive at Patreon already knowing who they want to support. The opportunity: what if Patreon could create that discovery moment itself?

**Search Limitations Block Discovery.** The existing search only works if you have a specific name or keyword. For users who want to browse by mood, genre, or vibe, there's nothing. The search bar is a dead end for exploration.

**Cross-Platform Journeys are Common.** Users start on one platform and branch out to others. They discover a creator on YouTube, follow them on Instagram, and eventually find their Patreon. The platforms users already live on hold the signal for what they'd want to discover next.

---

## Design Process & Key Decisions

**Redesigned Homepage.** The homepage becomes a discovery surface rather than a dashboard. Shorts-style content previews from creators give users a taste before they commit — borrowing the scroll-and-sample pattern from platforms where discovery already works.

**New Explore Page.** A dedicated browsing experience where patrons can explore creators by category, genre, and curated collections. Patrons are surfaced at the top for easy access.

**Account Linking.** Users can connect Spotify, Instagram, and other platform accounts. Their existing preferences and listening/viewing habits inform recommendations — so Patreon's suggestions feel like they already know you, because in a sense, they do.

**Sage — AI Discovery Guide.** Sage is a conversational AI that helps users articulate what they're looking for when they can't put it into words. Instead of browsing categories or typing keywords, users can describe a mood, interest, or type of content, and Sage surfaces relevant creators. It uses connected account data to curate recommendations that feel personal rather than algorithmic.

## Validation

**Sage Makes a Strong First Impression.** Users quickly grasped the purpose of Sage, describing it as "friendly" and "engaging" within moments of use — indicating strong onboarding and personality alignment.

**Connection Is Powerful — But Needs Clarity.** Participants appreciated the ability to link their Spotify or Instagram accounts but expressed concerns over data control, signaling a need for more transparent permissions and settings.

**Transparency Builds Trust.** A majority of testers wanted clearer explanations for why creators were recommended, highlighting the importance of explainability in AI-driven suggestions. The recommendation isn't enough — users want to know *why*.
